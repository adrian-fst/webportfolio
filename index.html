Software Development.

(Mainly Web Development, somewhat full-stack, & is still learning)

Even though my course major is related to multimedia arts, a great portion of our curriculum contained software development, when I started senior high school, I took an Information Communication Technology (ICT) track, during the duration of my senior high school I was taught the basic fundamentals of programming, we were taught with Java as our first language to be in line with the basics of OOP (Object Oriented Programming), I was also exposed to early SQL DBMS fundamentals that time, and I remember my first major program that I have written all by myself, is a simple school enrollment system that releases a simple registration form to be printed, I won’t lie but I forgot how to use Java and its swing UI today, but it was a nice educational experience on my end. For the 2nd, and last year of my senior high school, my final project for that academic year is an RFID-based attendance logging system that we never deployed, I remember it using C++, and a borrowed reader from our instructor, that project taught me more about database management systems and the structured query language, but then again, the program logic is not complicated for our young minds back then.

After graduating senior high school, this is when my course majoring started to be towards multimedia, during my freshman year in college, I’ve become familiar with the more detailed processes when it comes to software development, during this year, we were taught the fundamentals of SDLC, more DBMS, which led to RDBMS, though most of which are just introductory courses because our major projects that time was more into graphic designs and photography.

Going into my sophomore year (2nd Year), our courses became entangled, a great portion of the course curriculum is sided towards multimedia, yet we had our first ‘baby’ capstone project that time, leaving me both with multimedia projects and development projects. Our final capstone project for my 2nd year, is a web-based tire inventory and management system written in PHP which I also took the full-stack role for an external client, which unfortunately, we haven’t deployed due to the initial spike of nCoV (now Covid-19) on our country which caused the initial lockdowns, we have submitted it for them to use, but there was no proper turnover whatsoever.

For my final senior year (3rd Year), we were reintroduced to software engineering fundaments, as well as information assurance and security, our final capstone project which not surprisingly, I become as the team full stack programmer(again), is a complete web-based clinic appointment handling and clinic management for a small-scale clinic (JS, PHP), it is still undergoing tests, but is both approved by our thesis adviser, course facilitator, and the clinic owner.
